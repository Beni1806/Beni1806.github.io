Legalization in Switzerland (2025)

Switzerland is preparing to legalize cannabis for recreational use. In 2025, the Federal Council submitted a draft law for public consultation. The law focuses on safety, prevention, and controlled access.

What the Draft Law Proposes

  • Adults (18+) can legally buy and consume cannabis.
  • Sales only in licensed, quality-controlled shops.
  • Home growing allowed in small quantities per person/household.
  • No cannabis advertising permitted.
  • THC limits and health warnings mandatory on products.

The consultation lets cantons, parties, and citizens give feedback. The final version may change before it's voted on in Parliament. If passed, the law could take effect in 2026 or 2027.

Pilot Projects Continue

Until national legalization, pilot studies run in cities like Zurich and Basel. Selected adults can legally purchase cannabis under strict conditions to help study legalization’s effects.

What’s Legal Now?

For now, cannabis remains illegal outside of pilot projects. Possession of under 10g is decriminalized and usually fined 100 CHF. Selling or growing without permission can lead to legal charges.

Stay Informed

Switzerland’s plan focuses on public health and youth protection. To stay updated, follow trusted Swiss news sources or federal announcements as the situation continues to evolve.
